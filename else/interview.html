Virtual Interviews: First Impressions

  • you need to be the first to speak. Smile and say something positive. Start and end with thank you
  • appearance. Suit w/ dress pants and nice socks
  • body language. Sitting up straight. Looking for confident and friendly. SMILE
  • look at camera instead of yourself
    • move yourself directly at camera
  • clean up my desk and my surroundings
  • stay focussed
  1. Test tech
  2. Be early
  3. Start with a strong introduction, say hello and smile into camera
  4. Be prepared for positive small talk

How to come across

Appearance

  • have lights in front of you
  • put books below laptop so you're eye-level w/ camera
  • dress up
  • have wall behind you
  1. Sound - quiet space with some insulation
  2. Background - clean, simple, professional background
  3. Camera Angle - ensure camera is eye-level
  4. Lighting - natural light or a lamp
  5. Dress - appropriately from head to toe

Body Language

  • posture (positive body language demonstrates confidence)
  • eye contact
  • facial expressions
  • voice: speak clearly and at a good volume and pace. Explain any silences and avoid filler words. Don't speak quickly, take long, slow breaths while booting up computer
    • Employer wants complete answers, speak for about 1.5 mins
  • University of Maryland AI guide, hirevue 6 tips

How to answer questions

  • tell me about yourself. 3-5 highlights that are most relevant to the job or field. Paid/unpaid work, academics, and extracurricular activities. WHY is your background a great fit for the company?
    • how did you get interested in this industry?
    • relevant skills, knowledge and experiences
    • what have you done to develop these skills?
    • where are you now?
    • what are you excited about for the future?
    • don't only discuss work history because they can read that in resume
    • 90-120 seconds

Situation you had to juggle several tasks at once and how I prioritized work?

At Trylon, I would receive calls from customers while I'm working. At the beginning, I would answer it immediately in order to make the customer feel valued. However, calls would sometimes come in the middle of my modelling or design projects, and it would take a bit of time of refreshing memory to get back to my earlier works

Questions to ask at the end of the interview

  • if you have questions, it shows that you want to know more
  • what do you expect a co-op student to have accomplished in the first month
  • what aspects of the role do you believe to be the most rewarding or most challenging
  • key priorities for someone in this role?
  • types of projects previous co-op students was involved in?
  • what type of training would there be for successful candidate?
  • in what wyas can i prepare for this job, if I am the successful candidate?
  • ask questions that you are really curious about the company?
  • never ask negative questions!
  • ask about company culture? Not directly, but ask about most rewarding parts
  • when asking about COVID, ask how their role has changed due to COVID
