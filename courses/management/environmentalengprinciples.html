Environmental engineering is discipline of engineering devoted to the development and application of scientific knowledge through technology to remove, minimize, or prevent adverse effects associated with human activities. It involves removal, minimization, and prevention and reuse

The first step is identification, which includes discovering contaminants threating our health and nature. The next step, quantification, involves creating thresholds of contaminants over which human health can be seriously threatened, called maximum acceptable concentrations (MACs). Quantificaiton is an important process to ensure we are not disclosed to chemicals and pathogens over MACs to ensure protection of humans and nature. The next step, control, involves removing contaminants below MAC to protect humans and nature. Pollutant control means transformation of noxious forms of contaminants into stable, healthy forms. This indicates re-location of contaminants, but we should ensure that relocated contaminants are safe in solid and gas phases. Physical, chemical, and biological methods are used.

Contaminants and concentration

There are three types of contaminants: microbiological, chemical and physical, and radiological contaminants.

Pathogenic microorganimss and viruses are the two types of microbiological contaminants. There are 1400 known species of human pathogens, including viruses, bacteria, fungi, protozoa, and helminths. We cannot measure all of these because it is very expensive and technically challenging. Drinking water is tested for Escherichia coli as an indicator of faecal contamination and is required to have none per 100mL. Enteric viruses require 99.99% reduction (4 log) and protozoa require 99.9% reduction (99.9%).

Hazardous substances that physically and chemically affect human health must be kept below MAC. There are more than 20-80k chemicals being produced, but less than 5-10% are monitored. Drinking water has MAC of 0.2μgL0.2 \frac{\mu{g}}{L}0.2Lμg​ and 0.6μgL0.6 \frac{\mu{g}}{L}0.6Lμg​ for PFOA and PFOS, respectively. There are more than 50 types of PFAS compounds. While there are indicators for microbiological parameters, there are no indicator concepts for physical and chemical parameters

Natural and artificial radionuclides have MACs for drinking water as well. Radioactivity is measured in becquerel (Bq), with 1 Bq=1 event of radiation emission or disintegration per second.

Molar concentration definitions: Molarity (M) is the moles of solute per solution volume. Molality (m) is the moles of solute per kg of solvent. Mole fraction is moles of solute per total moles in system. Molecular weight: sum of atomic weight to 12C^{12}C12C in ideal systems

Equivalent concentration definitions: Normality is found by dividing the equivalent of solute by volume of solution. Equivalent of solute is found by dividing gram of solute by equivalent weight. Equivalent weight is found by dividing molecular weight of solute by change of charge or electrons, and has units g/eq. milliequivalent (meq) is the amount of millimoles comparing equivalent weight to mass present and g/mole

The equivalent in ionization of molecules, acid-base reactions (H+H^+H+ and OH−OH^-OH−), and reduction and oxidation (redox) reactions [electrons donated and accepted]. In reactions with multiple charges (acid-base), divide by the largest charge. In redox, divide by number of external electrons used in reaction.

Normality is commonly used because compounts in acid-base or redox reactions are normalized to "the number of charge or electron". Normality makes them olar ratio 1:1. For airborne reactions, correct mol/mol or v/v with temperature due to the PV=nRT relationships.

Also, alkalinity is less than lotal hardness if carbonate is less than other anions. Basic Water Chemistry and Water Quality Parameters

Oxygen Demand (OD): total amount of organic compounds expressed with O2O_2O2​. This is a theoretical amount of organics also called theoretical oxygen demand ThOD. Unit: mg/l O2. As hardness and alkalinity used CaCO3CaCO_3CaCO3​ equivalent, organics use O2O_2O2​ equivalent.

  • A high OD indicates high concentration of organics in water. This indicates organic contaminants in water, such as proliferation of microorganisms. This leads to lack of dissolved oxygen, pathogen issue, and impacts of aquatic animals. Impact is on water resource contamination, drinking water, ecoststems, and recreaetional activities
  • A low OD indicates low concentration of organics in water. This means less organic contaminants in water and less proliferation of microorganisms. This leads to less opportunities for lack of dissolved oxygen, pathogen, or aquatic animal impact. Water resource is protected, drinking water is great, and recreational activities are safe.

Procedure for OD calculation:

  1. Express the mole of organic compound as mole of electrons using half reactions
  2. Express mole of electrons with gram O2O_2O2​ using the O2O_2O2​ half reaction (0.25O2+H++e−=0.5H2O0.25O_2+H^++e^-=0.5H_2O0.25O2​+H++e−=0.5H2​O)
  3. Convert electrons to oxygen knowing 8g O2=1 mol e−8g\textrm{ }O_2=1\textrm{ mol }e^-8g O2​=1 mol e−

To build half reactions for organics:

  1. Balance an organic compound with CO2CO_2CO2​
  2. Balance OOO with H2OH_2OH2​O (or nitrogen with nitrogen source first)
  3. Balance HHH with H+H^+H+
  4. Balance charge with electron
  5. Express the reaction per mole of electrons as reduction form

Total solid (TS): residual matter in wet samples after dehydration. The separation of solids from water is one of the primary objectives of water and wastewater treatment, as solids contain organic and inorganic contaminants or pathogens. TDS is total dissolved solid (DS) in filtered samples; TSS is total suspended solid (SS) in non-filterable samples

  • Total volatile solids are the organic fraction of TS. Thus, VSS is volatile suspended solid. In burn, this goes to gas.
  • Total fixed solids are the inorganic fraction of TS. Thus, FSS is fixed suspended solid. In burn, this goes to ash and other solid.

Pathogen: disease-causing organism and microorganism, as well as virus

  • Pathogens present in water can transmit diseases through water
  • Water-related illness are the leading cause of human sickness and death worldwide
  • Examples of water-borne pathogens are Salmonella, Shigella, hepatitis, E.coli, Giardia lamblia, entamoeba histolytica, and cryptosporidium

E.coli is found in the intestinal tract of humans and warm-blooded animal, and thus is fecal contamination indicator. It is the most common aerobic bacteria (facultative bacteria). Thus, they use oxygen, but they can survive with or without oxygen. They do not require special environmental or nutritional conditions for growth. Although they are mostly not pathogenic, some are. E.coli O157:H7 is a strain from the cattle intestine that is pathogenic to humans and causes bloody diarrhea, abdominal cramp, and kidney failure.

Cryptosporidium (protozoa) is an enteric protozoan causing severe diarrhea and abdominal pain. It can last two weeks, and there is no reliable curative treatment fro cryptosporidiosis. These have "cysts" that are resistant to common treatment methods, like CL2CL_2CL2​, and thus UV disinfection or filtration is needed.

Giardia (protozoa) is a microscopic paraside that causes diarrheal illness giardiasis. Found on surfaces, in soil, food, or water contaminated with feces. It is protected by an outer shell that allows it to survive outside the body for long time and is tolerant to CL2CL_2CL2​. Commonly transmitted through drinking water

Virus has several waterborne types, like adenovirus, calicivirus, hepatitis A and E, rotavirus, norovirus, poliovirus. However, viruses are nono-sized particles containing nucleic acids (DNA or RNA), indicating frequent transmission through air as airborne viruses.
