Environmental engineering is discipline of engineering devoted to the development and application of scientific knowledge through technology to remove, minimize, or prevent adverse effects associated with human activities. It involves removal, minimization, and prevention and reuse

In last century, global temperature increased by 1ºC. Climate change impacts could be avoided by limiting global warming to 1.5ºC instead of 2ºC. This 1.5ºC goal requires large-reaching changes. Cabada's GHG emissions increased to 729 MtCO2 in 2018. Goal is 600MtCO2 by 2030. 15% reduction by 2020, 37% reduction by 2030. 50% reduction by 2050.

The first step is identification, which includes discovering contaminants threating our health and nature. The next step, quantification, involves creating thresholds of contaminants over which human health can be seriously threatened, called maximum acceptable concentrations (MACs). Quantificaiton is an important process to ensure we are not disclosed to chemicals and pathogens over MACs to ensure protection of humans and nature. The next step, control, involves removing contaminants below MAC to protect humans and nature. Pollutant control means transformation of noxious forms of contaminants into stable, healthy forms. This indicates re-location of contaminants, but we should ensure that relocated contaminants are safe in solid and gas phases. Physical, chemical, and biological methods are used.

Soil remediation technologies for treating organic, inorganic, and metal contaminants from soil and groundwater have evolved through three pathways with primary emphasis on:

  1. Extraction or gross removal processes with or without ex situ treatment (pump and treat)
  2. In situ (in place) remediation of contaminants
  3. Reliance on natural degradation and mass transfer processes

Contaminants and concentration

There are three types of contaminants: microbiological, chemical and physical, and radiological contaminants.

Pathogenic microorganimss and viruses are the two types of microbiological contaminants. There are 1400 known species of human pathogens, including viruses, bacteria, fungi, protozoa, and helminths. We cannot measure all of these because it is very expensive and technically challenging. Drinking water is tested for Escherichia coli as an indicator of faecal contamination and is required to have none per 100mL. Enteric viruses require 99.99% reduction (4 log) and protozoa require 99.9% reduction (99.9%).

Hazardous substances that physically and chemically affect human health must be kept below MAC. There are more than 20-80k chemicals being produced, but less than 5-10% are monitored. Molar concentration definitions: Molarity (M) is the moles of solute per solution volume. Molality (m) is the moles of solute per kg of solvent. Mole fraction is moles of solute per total moles in system. Molecular weight: sum of atomic weight to 12C^{12}C12C in ideal systems

Equivalent concentration definitions: Normality is found by dividing the equivalent of solute by volume of solution. Equivalent of solute is found by dividing gram of solute by equivalent weight. Equivalent weight is found by dividing molecular weight of solute by change of charge or electrons, and has units g/eq. milliequivalent (meq) is the amount of millimoles comparing equivalent weight to mass present and g/mole

The equivalent in ionization of molecules, acid-base reactions (H+H^+H+ and OH−OH^-OH−), and reduction and oxidation (redox) reactions [electrons donated and accepted]. In reactions with multiple charges (acid-base), divide by the largest charge. In redox, divide by number of external electrons used in reaction.
