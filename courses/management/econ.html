Lecture 1 - What is Economics & Engineering Decision Making

Defining Economics

Economics (1935): the science which studies human behaviour as a relationship between ends and scarce means which have alternative uses

Microeconomics: the study of how individual units (people or businesses) allocate their scarce resources, and the consequences of their decisions for markets and the entire economy

  • Welface economics is concerned w/ impacts of change on the well-being of various groups in the economy
  • Transportation economics is concerned w/ society's use of scarce resources for the movement of goods and people from point A -> B
  • Environmental economics is concerned w/ economic consequences of policies aimed at pricing pollution etc.
  • Ecological economics is concerned w/ placing value on nature over human needs
  • Urban economics is concerned w/ economic issues in cities like real estate markets, housing, poverty, and transportation. It has spatial dimension and agglomeration effects
  • Engineering economics is concerned w/ analyzes techniques to choose the best option from a bunch

Macroeconomics: the study of the economy as a whole, using simplified aggregate quantities to analyze the complete interactions of the economy

  • eg. GPA

Course objectives:

  1. Understand how to choose the most economically efficient project
  2. Understand when to make replacements
  3. Understand how to make public-sector choices

This understanding comes from:

  • cash flow analysis

Engineering Decision Making

  • projects use materials, money, labour, and time
  • projects can be undertaken in different ways
  • most economical is important
    • awareness of financial, environmental, social, and polical constraints is also essential

  • facts are used by influential personalities and powerful individuals to develop beliefs about what is best, leading to a decision
  • in theory, facts should control politics, people and preferences. However, sometimes a decision is made and facts are searched for after the fact
  • engineering economics provide facts about a decision

Dealing w/ Abstractions

  • simplifying the real world is necessary for any engineering analysis

  • model is a simplification, and is only appropriate for a given purpose
  • think about whether model assumptions are appropriate for a given purpose
  • if we include everything in the model, it is real world and overcomplicated. By definition, models are simplifications of the real world
  • we don't have precise values for some quantities
    • Sensitivity Analysis involves assessing the effect of uncertainty on a decision
    • a range of values can be assessed w/ reasonable certainty
    • accessing robustness is important to determine how sensitive decisions are to different factors in order to build confidence
