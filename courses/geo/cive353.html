Lecture 2 - Origin of soil

  • Chemical and boilogic sedimentary rocks susceptible to deformation and low strength
  • Chemical weathering can cause sinkholes
  • For water and wind-blown soils, the heaviest material is deposited first. Soils are susceptible to liquifaction
  • Due to the pressure of ice, ice depositd soils are the strongest
  • Shape affects strength
    • Sometimes, strength lost w/ lab because soil pressure and shape is lost. Thus, field testing is important
  • permanent deformation: inelastic
  • plastic: increase stress to certain level, strain increases with time when stress is constants
  • Soils with bigger voids are more susceptible to deformation
  • Behaviour is governed by particle size
  • Soils can behave as elastic and viscous materials
  • If silt has particles 75 microns or larger, it will behave as a sand. If smaller, it will behave as a plastic material like a clay. In nature, never exactly 75 microns. If sticky, acts as clay
