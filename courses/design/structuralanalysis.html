Lecture 1 - Introduction

Structural Design

  • governed by codes and standards
  • based on limit states design principles
    • ultimate and serviceability limit state
  • ontario building code obc
  • wood: CSA 086, steel: CSA 516, concrete: A23.3

Ultimate Limit State

  • Strength, Stability
    • includes fatigue (sometimes treated as a separate limit state)

Serviceability Limit State

  • deflections, vibrations
  • subjective criteria based on service loads/conditions

Loads

  • Permanent (dead)
    • self weight of structure
    • superimposed dead loads
  • Transient (live)
    • occupancy (residential 1.9 kPa, assembly 4.8, library 7.2, parking 2.4
    • snow
    • wind
    • earth or water pressure
    • earthquake
    • rain
    • temperature
    • strain-induced

Given information:

  • applied loads
  • imposed deformations
  • material properties (yield strength, rupture strength, E, G)
  • geometry (span, length, x-section)
  • boundary conditions (support conditions, joints)

Need to know:

  • internal forces/stresses
  • external reactions
  • deflections/deformations/rotations

Course assumptions:

  • static
  • linear-elastic behaviour/relatinship between stress and strain is known
  • small deformations/small rotations relative to member size/span
  • first order analysis
  • compatibility of strain
