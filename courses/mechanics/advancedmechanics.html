Course Outline

  1. Analysis of 3-D state of stress and strain in materials, Equilibrium equations, relationships between strain and displacement, strain compatibility
  2. 3-D stress-strain relationship for elastic solids (Hooke’s law). The limitations of elastic response, yielding of metals.The general considerations of #1, #2 will be applied to a number of different types of stress analysis problems that arise in practical applications. These include:
  3. St. Venant torsion of bars with non-circular cross-sections, including thin-walled sections
  4. 2-D Problems of plane stress and plane strain in polar coordinates: thick-walled cylinder under pressure (pipes, pressure vessels), composite pipes and shrink-fit problems
  5. Bending of thin flat plates (building construction, auto body panels,etc.)Suggested Reading:Full course notes will be posted to LEARN in the beginning of the term.

3D Stress System

The stress at a single point, say Q, is found using x, y, and z components. These components are all made up of 3 components themselves, for a total of 9 components. See below:

σ(y)=τyxi⃗+σyj⃗+τyzk⃗\sigma^{(y)}=\tau_{yx}\vec{i}+\sigma_y\vec{j}+\tau_{yz}\vec{k} σ(y)=τyx​i+σy​j​+τyz​k

σ(z)=τzxi⃗+τzyj⃗+σzk⃗\sigma^{(z)}=\tau_{zx}\vec{i}+\tau_{zy}\vec{j}+\sigma_{z}\vec{k} σ(z)=τzx​i+τzy​j​+σz​k

i,j, and k are components of the normal vector, N, that the stress σN\sigma_NσN​ goes through. Values of stress are inputted into the symmetric stress matrix

3D has applications for beams, torsion of bars, bending of plates, analysis of shells, and plane stress.

In 3D stress systems, sides exert the same distribution of force as applied onto it to maintain equilibrium. This resultant force is F divided by arbitrary area ΔA\Delta{A}ΔA. From here, you can see that axial dimensions approaching zero lead to the axial stress being equal to the negative of their prime counterparts. This demonstrates equal and opposite forces.
